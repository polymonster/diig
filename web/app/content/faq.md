# FAQ

## What is diig?

diig is a music discovery platform focused on electronic music. It aggregates releases from online record stores and Discogs, providing a smooth unified snippet listening experience to make music browsing online more enjoyable.

## Where is diig available?

diig is available online and as a native iOS / Android app.

## How do I get access?

diig is currently invite-only. You can register your interest below — leave a note if you like; if demand is high it may make a difference, though we don't want to be exclusionary.

## Why invite-only?

The website is in beta, the aim is to build a community around it slowly and to try and minimise the costs of running the platform.

## What does diig cost?

Nothing, for now. The website is not free to run, after passing 1000 users it will have to raise money, probably via a small subscription fee.

## What do I do with my invite code?

If you've been given an invite code, paste it in the message field when registering interest. We'll use it to fast-track your request.

## What kind of music is covered?

Primarily: techno, house, electro, tech house, minimal and bass music.

## Can I use Discogs with diig?

You can search Discogs and enjoy ad-free, responsive audio playback for quick digging, and add releases to your Discogs wantlist.

## Can I contribute?

Absolutely, diig is open source. You can open an issue or get involved on [GitHub](https://github.com/polymonster/diig).

## Can I sponsor the project?

Yes, you can support the project via GitHub [sponsors](https://github.com/sponsors/polymonster).

## Can I request features?

If you want particular record stores or music genres added to the platform we can take feature requests. Sponsors will get priority.

## I have a question not answered here?

Use the register interest form and include your question in the message — we'll get back to you.
